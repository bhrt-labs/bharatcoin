# BharatCoin — Questions for a US Crypto/Securities Lawyer

> Give this to a qualified lawyer admitted in your US jurisdiction *before* any public mainnet
> launch or accepting real money. Code cannot answer these — a lawyer must. This is not legal
> advice; it's a prep sheet so your first consultation is efficient.

## The facts a lawyer will need (hand these over)

- **What it is:** a fixed-supply ERC-20 token ("BHRT", 1,000,000,000 units, no mint function)
  on Ethereum / an Ethereum Layer-2, plus a Safe-based wallet configuration. No custom
  money-holding code.
- **Supply/allocation:** Community 64%, Staking 16%, Founder 17% (locked: 1-yr cliff + 3-yr
  linear vesting), Treasury 3%. (These are reserve buckets; **no** staking/yield program is
  offered, planned, or built.)
- **You:** a solo builder subject to US law, no company/entity yet, no outside funding.
- **Status:** nothing is live for value; plan is public testnet (no monetary value) → bug
  bounty → (only maybe) mainnet later. **No token sale / presale is planned or built.**
- **Not marketed as** an investment, a security, or "quantum-safe."

## The binary questions (answer these first — they can stop the project)

1. **Is BHRT a "security" / investment contract under the *Howey* test** (SEC) or any state
   blue-sky law? If yes, what registration/exemption/disclosure is required, and is it
   feasible for a solo builder?
2. **Does issuing or distributing a token make me a money transmitter / MSB** under FinCEN/BSA
   federal rules, or under **state money-transmitter law**? What registration/licensing
   applies, and in which states?
3. **KYC/AML obligations:** if people can convert BHRT to/from real money, what
   identity-verification and reporting am I legally required to do?
4. **Is a public *testnet* deploy (and open-sourcing the code) legally safe?** Tokens have no
   monetary value there — is there any exposure just from publishing/testing?

## Tax (IRS + state)

5. How does US tax law treat (a) me as issuer, (b) the founder-vesting allocation (income at
   grant vs. vest vs. sale?), and (c) future holders/users? Any **1099 / information-reporting**
   duty?
6. Do I have any **withholding/reporting** duty as the issuer or as an operator of any
   front-end?

## Structure & liability

7. Should I form a **company/entity** (an LLC — and in which state — or offshore) before doing
   anything public, to limit personal liability? Which structure fits a solo open-source crypto
   project?
8. What **disclaimers / terms of use / risk disclosures** must a front-end or website show?
9. If I open-source everything and someone else deploys it and loses money, **what's my
   liability**?

## Marketing / representations

10. My materials say "fixed supply, founder locked, not an investment, no quantum-safe claim."
    **Is that framing legally safe, and is there anything I must add or must not say** to avoid
    an implied offer of a security?
11. Is naming it "**Bharat**Coin" (evoking a nation) a legal/trademark/regulatory problem in
    the US?

## Founder allocation

12. Is a **17% founder allocation with 4-year vesting**, disclosed publicly, acceptable — or
    does it create any securities/insider implication I should handle differently?

## The one question that gates everything

> **"Given that I'm a solo builder subject to US law, and this is a fixed-supply token + a Safe
> wallet with no real money yet and no sale, what is the *minimum* I must legally do before
> (a) a public testnet, and (b) ever accepting real value — and is any of it a hard stop?"**

If the honest answer to (b) is "you can't do this solo/legally right now," that's vital to
know **before** investing more time — not after.
